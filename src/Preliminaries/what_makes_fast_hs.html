We’ll begin by showing small bite sized programs that demonstrate a particular way Haskell programs slow down. We call these programs canonical programs because each program is a micro example of a kind of slow down without any extra context, such as business logic. A reader should come away from this section with an understanding of the central ways a Haskell program slows down.
